Saeid A. Taghanaki

Principal Scientist & Manager, Microsoft Adjunct Research Professor, Simon Fraser University

I am a Principal Scientist & Manager at Microsoft, where I lead a team building agentic evaluation systmes for foundation-model systems. My research is anchored in one bet: as frontier models grow more capable, evaluation becomes the binding constraint on real-world reliability. That pulls me toward probing reasoning rather than pattern-matching, detecting reward hacking and memorization, and designing trajectory-, outcome-, and process-level signals that don't collapse under their own optimization pressure.

Before Microsoft, I worked on reliable AI at Autodesk Research and on ML/CV problems at early-stage startups. I completed my PhD in Computing Science at SFU under Prof. Ghassan Hamarneh, with research visits at MILA (Montréal) and Siemens Healthineers (Princeton).

Outside of work, I have a strong passion for soccer and the 100m sprint, with a few inter-university medals along the way.

Research Interests

My work sits at the intersection of evaluation, agentic systems, and foundation-model reliability. Three threads I keep pulling on:

  • Capability, reasoning, and self-evaluation; probing higher-order reasoning, shortcut learning, and capability elicitation in LLMs and VLMs; methods for models to evaluate themselves and each other (explanation–comprehension discrepancy, judge models) and the failure modes that emerge under optimization pressure.
  • Agentic and process-level evaluation; trajectory-, outcome-, and process-level signals for tool-using, long-horizon agents; evaluation pipelines that surface real failure modes without being gamed by the systems they measure.
  • Memorization, generalization, and robustness; detecting generative parroting, mitigating spurious correlations, and characterizing how foundation models behave under distribution shift.
